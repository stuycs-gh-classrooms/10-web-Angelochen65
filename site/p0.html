The Heart

The heart is a big muscle responsible for pumping blood through the rest of your body. It is responsible for sending blood to your organs. The diagram above shows the anatomy of the heart.

The heart has many components that makes it an effective pump of blood, which is necessary to transport oxygen to use in cellular respiration.

Atria and Ventricles

The heart is separated into 4 chambers. The top chambers are made up by the left and right atrium, and the bottom chambers are made of the left and right ventricles.

Blood first arrives through the veins into the atrium, then the blood flows through valves (so that blood does not blackflow) into the ventricles, where again they go through valves and are sent to parts of the body. The right side of the heart (our left, blue side) sends blood to the lungs to give the blood oxygen. The left side of the heart (the one on our right, the red) is responsible for sending blood with oxygen to the rest of the body.

Atria and Ventricles

The heart is separated into 4 chambers. The top chambers are made up by the left and right atrium, and the bottom chambers are made of the left and right ventricles.

Blood first arrives through the veins into the atrium, then the blood flows through valves (so that blood does not blackflow) into the ventricles, where again they go through valves then either go to different parts of the body. The right side of the heart (our left, blue side) sends blood to the lungs to give them oxygen. The left side of the heart (the one on our right, the red) is responsible for sending blood with oxygen to the rest of the body.

Arteries and Veins

Arteries and veins are blood vessles responsible for transporting blood

Arteries carry blood AWAY from the heart (a -> away), while veins carry blood to the heart. The major artery that sends blood to the body is the aorta, while the pulmonary arteries send blood to the lungs. The major veins that empty into the right atrium are the superior and inferior vena cava.
